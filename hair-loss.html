What is Hair Loss?

Hair Loss refers to reduced hair on the scalp. Every person experiences some hairfall (around 50-150 strands daily), but when hair grows back slower than it falls, that is known as hair loss. It occurs due to an imbalance in the Hair Growth Cycle.

The Growth Cycle

  • Anagen: Active growth phase.
  • Catagen: Transition phase.
  • Telogen: Resting phase.
  • Exogen: Shedding phase.

Common Causes

Our Ayurvedic experts have identified several factors that lead to premature thinning:

  • Poor blood circulation to the scalp.
  • Nutritional deficiencies (Lack of Iron/Protein).
  • Stress and Lack of sleep.
  • Hormonal imbalances.
